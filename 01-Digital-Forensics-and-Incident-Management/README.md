# Digital Forensics and Incident Management  

## Redacted Academic Case Study

This repository contains a redacted digital forensics and incident management
case study was produced for academic and professional development purposes.

The project demonstrates forensic evidence handling, artefact analysis,
ethical redaction, and responsible disclosure in line with UK GDPR and
professional digital forensics standards.

All materials have been reviewed and sanitised to ensure they are safe for
public, academic, and portfolio use.

---

## Learning Outcomes Demonstrated

This project demonstrates the following learning outcomes:

- Forensic acquisition and preservation of digital evidence
- Analysis of documents, images, and system artefacts
- Interpretation of browser history and user activity artefacts
- Timeline reconstruction and artefact correlation
- Identification of behavioural and intent indicators
- Application of ethical, legal, and GDPR-compliant disclosure practices
- Professional reporting and evidence presentation

---

**Forensic Tools**

**Standards and Principles**
- ACPO Principles of Digital Evidence
- UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018
- Evidence integrity and chain of custody
- Ethical disclosure and harm minimisation

---

## Scope and Limitations

This case study is based on the analysis of a single forensic image and related
artefacts. It does not include live system analysis, network traffic capture,
or memory forensics.

Findings are limited to the artefacts present within the provided dataset and
are interpreted strictly within an academic context.
